BBQ Burger

A barbecue burger is a flavorful twist on the classic hamburger that centers on a smoky, tangy, or sweet flavor profile achieved through specific ingredients and cooking techniques. The defining element is typically a rich barbecue sauce, which can be glazed over the patty or mixed into the ground beef‚Äîideally an 80/20 blend for maximum juiciness. These burgers are often piled high with "smokehouse" toppings such as crispy bacon, sharp cheddar cheese, and fried onion rings to add texture and depth. While most are seared quickly over a charcoal or gas grill to create a charred crust, true barbecue enthusiasts may slow-smoke the patties with wood chunks to deeply infuse them with authentic pit-cooked flavor.

Kitfo

Kitfo is a prized Ethiopian delicacy consisting of finely minced raw beef marinated in niter kibbeh (a herbed, clarified butter) and mitmita (a fiery chili-based spice blend). Originating from the Gurage people, this "Ethiopian steak tartare" is traditionally served completely raw, though it can be ordered lightly seared as leb leb for those who prefer a touch of heat. It is typically presented on a communal platter with ayib (mild cottage cheese) and gomen (collard greens) to balance the spice, and eaten using kocho or injera flatbread as a utensil.
